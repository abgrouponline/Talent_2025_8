RFI Response: Lone Worker Safety Solutions

1. Key Requirements from Caerphilly County Borough Council

The Council’s RFI indicates they are seeking information on solutions for lone worker safety for approximately 150 employees across departments such as Housing, Environmental Health, and Trading Standards. The key points include:

  • Existing system: The Council currently uses fob-style devices and mobile applications to monitor lone workers, with a contract running until April 2026.
  • Market understanding: They want to know what technologies and services are available for lone worker protection, including emerging and innovative solutions.
  • Scalability & integration: They are interested in solutions that can scale to additional users, integrate with existing IT systems, and offer a seamless user experience.
  • Supplier identification: The Council is gathering information to identify potential suppliers for future procurement opportunities.
  • Focus areas: Emphasis is on safety, reliability, ease-of-use, compliance, and the ability to respond quickly in emergencies.

In essence, the Council wants to explore the latest technologies that enhance lone worker safety while ensuring they fit into existing workflows and IT infrastructure.
